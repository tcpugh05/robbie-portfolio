    Robert Gregson is a NYC-based filmmaker. His films have been featured at Anthology Film Archives, Avignon Film Festival, and the Rappahannock Independent Film Festival. He writes, directs, edits, and shoots short and long-form media for clients such as Adweek, Anheuser-Busch, the Bnai Zion Foundation, as well as many independent businesses and organizations in NYC.

    Currently, he is in postproduction on Demon Fighters, a feature documentary he produced and directed with Shoebox Story Productions. He recently finished a fictional short film, Advice, and a short documentary, Just a Phase, and is in preproduction on his next short.

    Director/Producer
    Feature Documentary



    Demon Fighters tells the story of Demarcus Reed, a man who claimed to have a divinely inspired vision of his soulmate when he was 19. He spent the next 11 years as a celibate virgin searching for her around the world. A group of believers support his vision and the church he developed around it. The film follows his search as well as the complicated relationships he has with the people who join and leave the church.
